## 2. Self-registration — Keystone's structural advantage

**No Korean language institute or university requires an "authorized agent" to submit an application.** Portals (UWAY, Jinhak, universities' own systems) accept applications from whoever holds the student's documents. Keystone's value is document preparation, the apostille chain (Education Board → MOFA Dhaka), bank-statement compliance, and D-4→D-2 pathway planning.

Of the 88 institutions in the master list, **~70 have a direct self-apply route** (flagged in the "Self-Register?" column). Priority-A institutions with verified direct portals: Chungnam National (dream.cnu.ac.kr), Kyungsung (admissions.ks.ac.kr), Changwon National (jinhakapply.com), Sun Moon (UWAY), the whole K-CORE vocational cluster (own portals).

## 3. Rebranding strategy — "The Verifiable Consultancy"

### The market context (researched, sourced)
- CID estimates **~7,000 students cheated over 10 years** by consultancy fraud; headline cases (BSB Global Network — chairman arrested; Just Thought — Tk8.38 crore taken from 91 students, none sent) are **all Dhaka-based firms**.
- What authorities tell families to check: a physical office you can visit, bank-channel payments only, verifiable university relationships, and distrust of visa "guarantees."
- Comparable markets (Nepal's The Next Education with 13+ secondary-town branches; India's Tier-2 boom) show **hometown physical presence is itself the differentiator** against capital-city generalists.

### The positioning
**"আমরা লুকাই না — আমরা শেখাই।" (We don't hide — we teach.)**
Keystone as the *anti-Dhaka-agency*: the consultancy that teaches families to verify everything themselves.

Four pillars:
1. **Radical verifiability.** Every recommended university is IEQAS-certified — and we *show families how to check it themselves* on studyinkorea.go.kr. Turn Korea's own government system into our trust signal. No competitor does this; it directly counters the fraud fear.
2. **The founder's 9 years in Korea.** Stronger lived credibility than anything KUAC or PranBin publicly shows. Make it the brand story everywhere: "আপনার ছেলে-মেয়ে যেখানে যাচ্ছে, আমি সেখানে ৯ বছর ছিলাম।"
3. **Your town, our office.** Gazipur + Mymensingh + Narsingdi walk-in offices where the Dhaka firms have nothing. The headline frauds are Dhaka firms — a non-Dhaka brand can say "no bus ticket to Dhaka, no stranger with your money."
4. **Honest credentials only.** ApplyBoard Recruitment Partner status is real and independently vetted (~30% of applicants rejected) — badge it. **Do not** copy PranBin's "Korean government licensed" claim: research found **no Korean licensing scheme for foreign agents exists** (IEQAS certifies universities, not agencies), so that claim is unverifiable puffery — and publicly calling for verifiable credentials quietly neutralizes it.

### Trust mechanics to implement
- Publish the fee ladder (₹0 registration → pay-per-milestone → final payment after visa) on one page; bank/bKash receipts for every taka.
- Reframe "98% success" with its denominator, or drop it — guarantee-style claims now pattern-match to fraud warnings.
- Every enrolled student = one verifiable proof post (admit letter + university's IEQAS entry screenshot).
- Wall poster in each office: "এই ৫টি জিনিস যাচাই করুন — যেকোনো এজেন্সিতে" (Check these 5 things — at any agency). Being the agency that teaches suspicion wins the suspicious market.

## 4. Reducing friction — the tooling plan (from GitHub research)

**Honest finding:** no mature open-source "study-abroad agency" product exists — everything in that exact niche is a zero-star portfolio project. The play is a generic tool configured to our pipeline.

| Stage | Tool | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Now (replace Google Sheets)** | **NocoDB** (github.com/nocodb/nocodb, 64k★, Airtable-style, self-hosted) | Same mental model as Sheets, plus: student self-service intake forms, file-attachment columns for passport/transcripts/bank docs, checkbox columns for the apostille chain, Kanban by pipeline stage, webhooks to the WhatsApp bot. One developer-day (Fahim) to deploy on a small VPS; daily use needs zero technical skill. |
| **Fallback if only shared hosting** | **EspoCRM** (3.1k★, PHP) | Runs on cheap cPanel hosting with a web installer — no Docker at all. |
| **WhatsApp (later)** | Keep `bots/wa_bot_marina.js` short-term → **Evolution API + Chatwoot** when volume outgrows it | Shared team inbox (Marina + founder) with the student record attached to every chat. Note: unofficial WhatsApp clients carry account-ban risk — plan for the official Cloud API eventually. |
| **Do NOT adopt** | Twenty CRM, docassemble, any 0-star "visa CRM" repo | Need a retained developer; overkill for a 3-person team. |

**The pipeline, end to end:** NocoDB intake form (Bangla) shared via Facebook/WhatsApp → auto-creates student record → document checklist with per-item status → WhatsApp bot reminders for missing docs (small webhook script) → application submitted via university portal (tracked in Kanban) → visa stage → arrival. Every step visible to the family on request — which is itself the brand.
