## 3. The core fear = visa security (your product to students)

Every Bangladeshi family's real question is: *"Will my child actually get the visa?"* Your answer, and your moat, is a **visa-security system**:

1. **Target regional-visa-capable universities** — steer students to programs with the ~95% D-4-7 / regional-visa success profile (KDU Global via KUAC is your proven lane).
2. **Pre-submission document audit** (`audit-system/`) — every file is machine-checked before it's sent, so it clears on the first pass (no name mismatches, no expired bank statements).
3. **Founder's 9 years in Korea** — real process knowledge, not a broker's guesswork.

Frame to WeBring: *the students we send you have already cleared the highest-risk hurdle — that makes them your ideal, lowest-cost customer.* That is what earns you good terms.

> ⚠️ Honesty note for your own pitch: "100% visa" is a **process guarantee, not a legal one** — no one can promise a sovereign visa outcome. Sell it as "we maximize visa security by only sending students to regional-visa-capable universities and auditing every document to zero-defect before submission." That's true, defensible, and still the strongest promise in the market.

## 6. Why each move works (use these live in the call)

- **You accepted the meeting but immediately set the frame** — visa-secured students — so the conversation starts on your strength, not as a supplicant.
- **You named that you do admissions yourself** — this quietly raises your status (you're not a naive lead) and forces her to articulate her real value, which is the information you actually need.
- **The pricing question is wrapped in "a fair model for both sides"** and paired with a specific guess — she'll correct a guess far more freely than answer "what do you charge," and you learn the structure without looking like you're shopping.
- **You tied your students to lower risk/cost for her** — planting, before any number is named, that your inbound deserves discount-worthy terms.
- **You never oversold** — she approached you; curiosity and confidence beat a hard pitch.
