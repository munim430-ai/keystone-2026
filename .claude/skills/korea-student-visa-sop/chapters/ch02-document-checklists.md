# Chapter 2: Document Checklists — Online Application, DHL Courier, and Medical

## Core Idea
There are two distinct document sets — the digital set for the online application and the notarized physical set for DHL courier to Korea — plus a small medical set for the TB test; preparing them in parallel prevents the classic two-week stall.

## The Online Application Set (EAP, KLP, Bachelor & Masters — universal)
1. Passport (color copy)
2. Photo (Korean size 35×45 mm, clear)
3. Own NID & parents' NID
4. Family Relation Certificate (FRC)
5. All academic certificates & transcripts (SSC, HSC or Diploma & Bachelor)
6. Language certificate (IELTS, TOEFL, TOPIK)
7. Personal statement & purpose of study (SOP)
8. Work experience certificate (if available — for Master's applicants; some universities require)
9. Letter of recommendation (some universities require for masters)
10. TIN, Trade & Bank (some universities require sponsor info)

## The DHL Physical Set (courier to the university in Korea)
1. All academic certificates and transcript — **ONLINE ATTESTATION FROM MY GOV BD**
2. Passport — 1 color copy + **notary**
3. Parents' NID / Birth Certificate — main copy's color copy + **translated copy, then notary**
4. Applicant's NID + Birth Certificate — color copy + translated copy, **then notary**
5. IELTS color copy — **notarized**
6. Korean-size photos — **5 copies, 35×45 mm**
7. Family Relation Certificate — color copy + **notarized**
8. Employment Certificate / Trade licence — **translated then notarized**
9. e-TIN & TAX certificate & acknowledgement certificate — **translated then notarized**
10. Bank statement + solvency — **no notarization needed**
11. SOP — **no notarization needed**

## The Medical Set (TB test — embassy stage)
- Main passport + 2 passport photocopies + 2 passport-size pictures
- Test hours 8 AM–6 PM; report delivered after 24 hours
- Named clinic in source: Prova Health Care, Plot 9, Road 17, Block C, Banani, Dhaka 1213

## Decision Rules
- **Translate then notarize — in that order** — for NID, birth certificates, employment/trade, and tax documents.
- **Never notarize** the bank statement, solvency certificate, or SOP — wasted money and time.
- **TIN/TAX/Trade are NOT mandatory for Kyungsung** — submit only "for safety purposes" if the family wants.
- Some universities (Sejong) require **e-apostilled** academic documents — check the university sheet before attestation.

## Anti-patterns
- **Notarizing everything indiscriminately**: the checklist marks exactly which items need notary — over-processing costs the family money and days.
- **Skipping translation before notarization**: a notarized Bangla document is still unusable; the sequence is translate → notarize.

## Key Takeaways
1. Build both sets simultaneously — online set unblocks submission, DHL set unblocks the CoA.
2. Attestation of academics is ONLINE via My Gov BD; physical attestation queues are unnecessary.
3. Keep 5+ Korean-size photos in stock per student; they recur at every stage.
