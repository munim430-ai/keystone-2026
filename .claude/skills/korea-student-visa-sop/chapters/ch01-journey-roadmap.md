# Chapter 1: The 10-Step Journey — Bangladesh to a Korean Classroom

## Core Idea
Every successful Korea admission follows the same 10-step sequence; the counsellor's job is to know which step a student is on and what unblocks the next one.

## The 10-Step Pipeline
1. **Start of application process** — gather documents and send swiftly
2. **Application fee payment** — the family's first financial commitment (80,000–170,000 KRW depending on university/level)
3. **Application submission** — formal submission of documents (online portal + DHL where required)
4. **Interview (if required)** — online via virtual platforms; some universities on-spot; mock interview mandatory
5. **Admission results** — notification of admission status
6. **Invoice & offer letter** — receipt of the financial and academic offer
7. **Tuition fee payment** — pay tuition; **must send SWIFT copies of the payment to the university**
8. **Certificate of Admission (CoA) issuance** — the official CoA; this is the visa trigger
9. **Embassy interview (visa application)** — attend the visa interview at the Korean Embassy, Dhaka
10. **Visa approval & fly to Korea**

## Key Concepts
- **CoA (Certificate of Admission)**: issued by the university only after documents are verified and tuition is paid — nothing at the embassy happens without it.
- **SWIFT copy**: proof of the international tuition transfer; universities require it before issuing the CoA.
- **E-VISA vs Normal Visa track**: some programs (Kyungsung, Gachon, Dongshin, aSSIST masters) are processed as e-visa; others (e.g. Sejong undergraduate) as normal embassy visa.
- **Regional Visa**: a restricted-major visa category (e.g. Kyungsung's Global Engineering majors; Sejong's IT college) tied to regional programs.

## Mental Models
- Think of the pipeline as **two gates**: the university gate (steps 1–8, ends at CoA) and the embassy gate (steps 9–10). Different documents, different failure modes.
- Money moves three times: application fee → tuition → (embassy visa fee). Prepare the family for all three up front.

## Anti-patterns
- **Starting the bank statement too early**: financial documents age; time them against the submission date, not the inquiry date.
- **Treating the interview as optional prep**: the trial/mock interview is mandatory in this process, not a suggestion.

## Key Takeaways
1. Track every student against the 10 steps; the step number IS the status report.
2. CoA is the single most important artifact — everything before it serves it, everything after depends on it.
3. SWIFT copies must be sent proactively; families don't know this and it silently stalls CoA issuance.
