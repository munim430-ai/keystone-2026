# Chapter 9: Interview Preparation — University & Mock Interview Protocol

## Core Idea
The interview is won on logistics discipline (display name, dress, connection) plus rehearsed answers to a known 23-question bank — and the mock interview is mandatory, not optional.

## The Display-Name Rule
Set the video-call display name from the passport, **surname FIRST, then given name** (ABIDA SIDDIQUA → "SIDDIQUA ABIDA"). If an Application ID is required, include it with the display name. This is the first thing checked before the interview even begins.

## Logistics Protocol
1. **Formal dress code** — boys: formal shirt and tie; overall neat and professional.
2. **Stable internet** — strong connection, no interruptions.
3. **Neat, clean, quiet background.**
4. **Prepared answers** — work through the question bank in advance, in the student's own words.

Plus: mock/trial interview attendance is **mandatory**; most interviews are online, but some universities interview on-spot; keep academic documents, IELTS certificate, and passport physically ready during the call.

## The 23-Question Bank (grouped by intent)
**Identity & motivation:** Introduce yourself · What major did you choose? · Why this major? · Why South Korea? · Why this university? (bank names Kyungsung — adapt) · What is your future plan?
**Credibility probes:** Good GPA but why no university admission yet? · How did you score 100% in HSC? · Why is your IELTS so low (5.5–6.5)? · What is your weakness? / strength?
**Funding & ties:** Who will be your sponsor? · What does your father do? · Any plans for a part-time job during studies?
**Subject checks — CSE/IT applicants:** What is software and hardware? · What is data? · What is a function? · Do you know any programming languages?
**Subject checks — business applicants:** What is Business? · What is Management?
**Culture:** Tell us about your country's culture. · Will the subject benefit you in the job market? · What is the purpose of studying your subject?

## Decision Rules
- Prep the subject-check set matching the student's major (Q11–14 for CSE/IT, Q18–19 for business) — skip the other.
- Every credibility-probe answer must match the SOP's version of events (Ch 6) — the officer reads the SOP first.
- Part-time job answers: acknowledge study-first intent; never make part-time income sound like the plan for funding (that contradicts the sponsor's affidavit).
- "Who is your sponsor?" must produce the exact same name as the financial affidavit and bank statement.

## Anti-patterns
- **Skipping the mock interview** — it's mandatory in this process; treat absence as a pipeline blocker.
- **Memorized-essay delivery** — the bank says prepare "in your own thinking"; recited answers trigger deeper probing.

## Key Takeaways
1. Half the interview is decided before the first question: display name, dress, background, connection.
2. The question bank is knowable — there is no excuse for a surprised student.
3. Consistency across SOP, cover letter, affidavits, and interview answers is the real subject of the interview.
