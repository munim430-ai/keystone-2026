# Chapter 8: The Two Notarized Affidavits — NOC and Financial Declaration

## Core Idea
Two stamp-paper instruments executed before a Notary Public in Dhaka carry the family's legal weight in the visa file: the parents' No-Objection affidavit and the sponsor's combined financial declaration — both must end with the return-to-Bangladesh undertaking.

## Instrument 1 — Affidavit of No Objection (NOC)
- **Form:** 2 pages on non-judicial stamp paper (৳100 first page + ৳50 continuation), notary seals, registration number, photos of both parents (and student) affixed and stamped across.
- **Deponents:** BOTH parents — full name, NID number each, joint permanent address (village/road, post office, police station, district), religion, nationality "Bangladeshi by birth."
- **The 5 clauses:** (1) we are permanent residents of Bangladesh; (2) [student] is our child — DOB, passport number — who desires higher studies at [university], Republic of Korea; (3) accepted for [program] in [semester], we agreed, classes start [month/year], we proudly encourage him/her; (4) **we have no objection** to our child going to Korea for higher education; (5) **he/she will return to Bangladesh after completion.**
- **Closing:** truth declaration → both parents sign → advocate/notary attests "the deponents are known to me and signed in my presence."

## Instrument 2 — Sponsorship & Financial Declaration and Property Valuation
- **Form:** 3 pages, stamp paper, notarized, executed by the sponsor (typically the father), photo affixed. Combines THREE functions: sponsorship declaration + property valuation + income declaration.
- **Deponent identification:** name, DOB, parents' names, NID, **TIN with taxes circle and zone**, address, religion, profession with business name, nationality.
- **Clauses:** citizen-by-birth with "strong family bonding, social and economical ties in Bangladesh" (tie strength = return credibility) → child's identity and acceptance at [university/program] → **acceptance notification received, dated, with application ID, and "I have deposited USD [X] as tuition fees & confirmed his place"** → properties free of encumbrance, saleable/transferable → solvent for ALL expenditures (tuition + living) for the full course.
- **Three tables:** (A) Movable properties — bank/branch, account no., balance in BDT and USD; (B) Immovable properties — flats/land/shops with full deed metadata (Deed No., District, Police Station, Sub-Registry, Mouza, J.L. No., Khatian C.S./S.A./R.S., Dag/Plot No.), market value BDT + USD; (6) Annual incomes — business name, trade license no., location, annual profit BDT + USD.
- **Conversion line under every table:** "1 USD = 122.00 BDT (as on [date]); Source: www.bb.org.bd."
- **Clause 7:** "my son will definitely come back to Bangladesh after completion of his studies without delay."

## Decision Rules
- Sponsor's name spelling must match the bank documents **exactly** — Gachon states a spelling mistake is grounds for rejection; apply that standard everywhere.
- Include the tuition-deposit clause whenever tuition is already paid — it is the strongest single credibility line in the file.
- Pair every BDT figure with USD at a dated, sourced exchange rate — officers should never have to convert.
- Full deed metadata for land (Khatian, Dag, Mouza) is what separates a verifiable claim from an assertion — collect it from the family early; it's the slowest item.

## Anti-patterns
- **One-parent NOC**: the sample uses both parents as joint deponents — a single signature invites questions.
- **Omitting the return clause** from either instrument — it is standard and its absence is conspicuous.

## Key Takeaways
1. Both instruments end the same way — return to Bangladesh — because both exist to answer the overstay question.
2. The financial affidavit is a 3-in-1; don't produce three separate documents.
3. Stamp paper, photos affixed, notary registration — form errors are as fatal as content errors.
