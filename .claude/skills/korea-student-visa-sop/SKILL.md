## Core Rules & Mental Models

**The Two-Gate Pipeline.** Every case passes two gates: the university gate (documents → app fee → submission → interview → admission → tuition + SWIFT copy → **CoA**) and the embassy gate (cover letter + affidavits + TB test → visa interview → fly). The CoA is the visa trigger; nothing at the embassy happens without it.

**Two document sets, prepared in parallel.** The *online set* (passport, Korean-size photo, NIDs, FRC, academics, language cert, SOP) and the *DHL physical set* (notarized/translated copies couriered to Korea). Chain rule: **translate → then notarize** for identity/tax/employment docs. **Never notarize** bank statement, solvency, or SOP. Academic certificates: online MyGovBD attestation for the DHL set — but **Sejong requires e-apostille** (a different chain).

**One story, three places.** Gap years, sponsor identity, funding, and return plan must be identical across SOP, cover letter, affidavits, and interview answers. Divergence is the most common self-inflicted rejection. The sponsor's name must match bank records **character-for-character** (Gachon rejects on a spelling mistake).

**The bank floor.** USD 20,000 held 6 months is the standard (Kyungsung, Sejong, Gachon, aSSIST); Dongshin accepts $16k–20k. Gachon additionally demands annual income ≥ 20,00,000 BDT with parents' income certificate and verifies study gaps via job certificates.

**Every document ends with return.** The NOC (both parents, stamp paper, notarized) and the 3-in-1 financial affidavit (sponsorship + property valuation + income, with USD conversions sourced to bb.org.bd) both close with the return-to-Bangladesh undertaking — as does a good SOP. All three exist to answer the overstay question.

**The weakness-reframe.** Weak HSC, low IELTS, gap years: name it → honest cause → corrective action → measurable improvement. Silence reads as hiding; the interview question bank explicitly probes these ("Why is your IELTS so low?").

**Scholarship-ladder arithmetic.** Compare total cost, not sticker: 1-semester ladders (Kyungsung/Sejong/Dongshin/Gachon) vs multi-semester (aSSIST 50%×3 sem; Kyungsung masters 40% guaranteed at GPA 3.0+). One IELTS band step can be worth more than any negotiation (Dongshin 6.0→6.5 = 40%→50%).

**Interview = logistics + consistency.** Display name in passport order (SURNAME GivenName + App ID), formal dress, clean background, stable connection, documents at hand, mandatory mock attended — then rehearsed (not recited) answers to the known 23-question bank, subject-set chosen by major.
